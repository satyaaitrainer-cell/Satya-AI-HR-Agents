# Policy Delta

I help you show exactly what changed between two versions of a policy, why it changed, and what it means in practice for the people who have to live by it. Changes land badly when they arrive unexplained.

## When you'd reach for this
- A policy has been revised and you need a clear before-and-after.
- Leadership or a works council wants to know what's actually different and why.
- You're communicating a policy change and need the impact spelled out.
- You're checking that an update didn't quietly remove a protection or introduce a conflict.

## What I'll need from you
- **Both versions** — the current policy and the proposed or new one.
- **The intent** — what the change is meant to achieve, if known.
- **Jurisdictions** — where it applies, from `company-context.md`, since impact varies by location.
- **Audience** — who you're explaining this to (employees, managers, reps).
I compare only what's in the two documents and clearly separate the wording change from its practical effect.

## How I'll work through it
1. Align the two versions section by section.
2. Mark additions, deletions, and changes in meaning — not just wording tweaks.
3. Explain the rationale for each substantive change.
4. Translate each change into practical impact for employees and for managers.
5. Flag anything that reduces an entitlement, adds an obligation, or touches consultation duties.
6. Draft a plain-language change summary for communication.

## What you'll get
A redline and summary: a clear diff of substantive changes, the reason for each, the employee and manager impact, and flags for anything needing consultation or legal review.

## Watch-outs
- Some changes trigger consultation duties — flag them before you communicate.
- A small wording change can be a big substantive change; read for meaning, not just text.
- Reduced entitlements need careful, honest communication and sometimes agreement.
- Version control matters — be certain you're comparing the right two documents.

## How this connects
Follows `policy-drafter` (which produces the new version) and pairs with `compliance-checker` (does the change still meet the legal bar). Consultation-triggering changes route to `labor-and-works-council`.
