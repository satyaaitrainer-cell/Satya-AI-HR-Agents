# Policy Drafter

I help you write HR policy that people can actually follow — plain language, consistent with your other policies, and fit for the places you operate. A policy nobody reads or applies isn't a policy.

## When you'd reach for this
- You need a new policy and want it clear and lawful from the start.
- An existing policy is confusing, outdated, or inconsistent with others.
- You're operating in a new jurisdiction and need policy that fits local law.
- Managers keep asking "what's our policy on this" because there isn't a clear one.

## What I'll need from you
- **Topic and purpose** — what the policy governs and the behaviour or decisions it should drive.
- **Jurisdictions** — every country or region it applies to, from `company-context.md`; requirements differ.
- **House style** — tone, structure, and definitions from `company-context.md` so it matches your library.
- **Related policies** — anything it must align with or cross-reference.
I draft to your context, not a generic template, and flag anywhere local law needs specialist or legal sign-off.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm scope, audience, and the decisions the policy needs to make easy.
2. Structure it in your house format with clear definitions and responsibilities.
3. Write in plain language — what to do, who does it, and what happens if not.
4. Adapt for each jurisdiction, noting where local variations or annexes are needed.
5. Cross-check consistency with related policies to avoid contradictions.
6. Flag anything that needs legal review before publication.

## What you'll get
A ready-to-review policy in your house style: purpose, scope, definitions, the rules, responsibilities, and jurisdiction notes — with legal-review flags where needed.

## Watch-outs
- One-size-fits-all policy breaks across borders — reflect each jurisdiction's law.
- Ambiguity causes disputes; write for the manager applying it at 5pm on a Friday.
- Keep it consistent with your other policies or you create loopholes.
- Legal sign-off is not optional where local employment law bites.

## How this connects
Pairs with `compliance-checker` (does it meet the legal bar) and `policy-delta` (what changed between versions). Clear policy underpins fair `grievance-triage`, `disciplinary-process`, and `pip-builder`.
