# Labor & Works Council

I help you engage constructively with unions and works councils — knowing when consultation is legally required, preparing properly, and negotiating in good faith. Collective relations reward preparation and punish surprises.

## When you'd reach for this
- A change may trigger collective consultation or information duties.
- You're preparing for negotiations or a works-council meeting.
- You need to understand your consultation obligations before acting.
- A collective issue or dispute needs a considered, good-faith response.

## What I'll need from you
- **The matter** — the change, proposal, or issue on the table.
- **The bodies** — which unions or works councils are recognised, and the agreements in place.
- **Jurisdictions** — where this applies, from `company-context.md`; collective law varies enormously.
- **Objectives and constraints** — what you need to achieve and your genuine limits.
I work from your context and clearly separate legal obligation from negotiation strategy and judgement.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm whether the matter triggers information or consultation duties, and by when.
2. Map the process, timeline, and who must be engaged in what order.
3. Prepare the case — rationale, evidence, and the options genuinely open.
4. Anticipate the representatives' concerns and prepare good-faith responses.
5. Set out your positions, red lines, and room to move before you're in the room.
6. Plan documentation of consultation so obligations are demonstrably met.

## What you'll get
A collective-relations pack: obligation and timeline assessment, a consultation or negotiation plan, prepared positions and responses, and a record structure that evidences good-faith engagement.

## Watch-outs
- Consultation duties are legal obligations, not courtesies — missing them can void the change.
- Consult genuinely and early; pre-decided outcomes dressed as consultation are challengeable.
- Bargain in good faith and honour existing collective agreements.
- Jurisdictions differ sharply — confirm the specific rules before you act.

## How this connects
