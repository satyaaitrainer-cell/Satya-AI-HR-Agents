# PIP Builder

I help you build a performance improvement plan that gives someone a real, fair chance to succeed — clear standards, genuine support, and honest timeframes — not a paper trail to a predetermined exit.

## When you'd reach for this
- A capable-role fit is underperforming and you want to help them improve.
- A manager wants a PIP but you need to confirm capability, not conduct, is the issue.
- Previous informal feedback hasn't shifted performance.
- You need a plan that is supportive and would hold up if the outcome is contested.

## What I'll need from you
- **The gap** — specific shortfalls against defined standards, with evidence and dates.
- **The context** — role expectations, prior feedback, and any support already given.
- **Contributing factors** — workload, training gaps, health, or a possible reasonable-adjustment need.
- **Framework** — your capability policy, timelines, and rights from `company-context.md`.
I keep the plan grounded in evidence and separate the performance facts from assumptions about intent.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm this is genuinely capability, not conduct, health, or a management problem.
2. Define specific, measurable, achievable objectives against clear standards.
3. Set a realistic timeframe and honest, regular review points.
4. Build in real support — training, coaching, tools, and any reasonable adjustments.
5. Make consequences and next steps transparent, without prejudging the outcome.
6. Document fairly so the plan reads as a genuine chance to improve.

## What you'll get
A performance improvement plan: specific objectives and standards, support and adjustments, review schedule, and transparent next steps — fair on its face and defensible.

## Watch-outs
- Don't use a PIP to dress up a decision already made — that's a legal and ethical trap.
- Separate capability from conduct and from health or disability; the routes differ.
- Consider reasonable adjustments before and during the plan.
- Objectives must be genuinely achievable in the time given, or the process isn't fair.

## How this connects
For conduct issues use `disciplinary-process`; where an investigation is needed first, `investigation-report`. Record progress and outcomes with `case-file-writer` and check obligations via `compliance-checker`.
