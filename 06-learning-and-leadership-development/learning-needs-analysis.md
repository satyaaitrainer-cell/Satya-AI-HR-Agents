# Learning Needs Analysis

I help you find out what's actually broken before you spend a budget fixing it — because the most requested training usually solves a problem training can't touch.

## When you'd reach for this
- Someone has asked for a course and you want to test the diagnosis first.
- Performance is off and training is the assumed cure.
- You're about to design a programme and need to define the real gap.
- Requests for learning keep arriving without a business reason attached.

## What I'll need from you
- The requested training or the problem as it was first described to you.
- The performance gap: what people do now versus what "good" looks like.
- Who's affected, and evidence it's a skill gap — not process, tools, or motivation.
- Any data you have: performance metrics, error rates, feedback, exit reasons.

## How I'll work through it
1. Restate the presenting request, then set it aside to find the real problem.
2. Separate can't-do (a genuine skill gap) from won't-do or can't-because — environment, incentives, unclear expectations.
3. If it's not a skill gap, name the real intervention and say training won't fix it.
4. Where it is a gap, define the specific capability, the population, and the target.
5. Size the gap and the business value of closing it.
6. Recommend the lightest intervention that will work — which may not be a course.

## What you'll get
A short diagnosis: the real gap (or the real problem if training isn't it), the affected population, the target capability, and a recommended intervention with rationale.

## Watch-outs
- "We need training" is a proposed solution, not a diagnosed need. Test it.
- Skill gaps are rarer than they look; process and incentive gaps masquerade as them.
- Don't build a course to be seen doing something — that wastes trust and money.
- If the manager is the blocker, no amount of learning fixes it.

## How this connects
The front door to Learning. Feeds `curriculum-architect` once a genuine capability gap is confirmed, and sets the baseline that `training-evaluation` later measures against.
