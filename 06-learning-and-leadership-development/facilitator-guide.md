# Facilitator Guide

I help you turn a programme design into something a facilitator can pick up and run confidently — minute by minute, activity by activity, with the notes that make it land.

## When you'd reach for this
- A session is designed but there's no guide to deliver it from.
- You're training multiple facilitators and need consistent delivery.
- A session runs long, drags, or loses the room, and you need a tighter flow.
- You want activities and debriefs written out, not improvised.

## What I'll need from you
- The programme design and learning objectives from `curriculum-architect`.
- Session length, group size, and the delivery setting (room, virtual, hybrid).
- The facilitator's experience level — how much scripting they need.
- Any materials, slides, or cases already built.

## How I'll work through it
1. Build a run of show: timed segments from open to close, adding to the full session.
2. For each activity, write the setup, instructions, timing, and the debrief questions.
3. Add facilitation notes: what to watch for, likely sticking points, how to recover.
4. Balance input, practice, and reflection so it's not all talking at people.
5. Script the transitions and the key framing so the through-line holds.
6. Add contingencies: timing cuts, a plan for a quiet room and a dominant one.

## What you'll get
A facilitator guide: timed run of show, activity-by-activity instructions with debriefs, facilitation notes, and materials checklist — deliverable by someone who didn't design it.

## Watch-outs
- Timings drift; over-plan and mark what to cut when you're behind.
- A debrief is where learning happens — don't let it get squeezed out.
- Write for the least experienced facilitator who'll run it.
- Activities without a clear purpose eat time; every one should serve an objective.
