# Leadership Development

I help you build leaders at every level — from first-time managers to senior leaders — with programmes matched to the very different jobs each transition demands.

## When you'd reach for this
- New managers are promoted for craft and left to sink or swim.
- You need a pipeline, not a one-off course, across leadership levels.
- Manager quality is uneven and it's showing up in engagement and attrition.
- Senior leaders need development too, not just the early-career cohort.

## What I'll need from you
- The leadership levels in your org and the transitions between them (`company-context.md`).
- Where the pain is: which transition is failing, and how you know.
- Your leadership expectations or competencies, if they exist.
- Cohort size, budget, and time available per level.

## How I'll work through it
1. Map the pipeline: the shift in mindset and skill at each leadership passage.
2. Prioritise the transition that's hurting most — usually the jump to first-line manager.
3. Define the capabilities that matter at each level, distinctly.
4. Design cohort programmes with practice, peer learning, and real-work application.
5. Weave in the manager basics: feedback, coaching, difficult conversations, calibration.
6. Build the reinforcement — manager-once-removed involvement, follow-through, measurement.

## What you'll get
A leadership development architecture: the pipeline by level, prioritised programmes, capability definitions, and a plan for practice and reinforcement, not just delivery.

## Watch-outs
- The craft-to-management jump is the biggest and the most neglected — invest there.
- One generic leadership course for all levels serves none of them.
- Development without on-the-job practice and a supportive boss rarely transfers.
- Don't forget senior leaders; they set the culture everyone else copies.
