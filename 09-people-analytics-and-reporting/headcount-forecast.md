# Headcount Forecast

I help you turn assumptions about growth, attrition, and hiring into a headcount and cost projection you can defend in a budget meeting.

## When you'd reach for this
- Annual or quarterly headcount and cost planning.
- Modelling the impact of a hiring plan, freeze, or reorg.
- Working out backfill needs given expected attrition.

## What I'll need from you
- **Starting headcount** by team/grade, and current cost.
- **Assumptions** — growth targets, attrition rate, time-to-fill, planned hires/exits.
- **Cost inputs** — average salary by grade, on-costs, joining timing.
No invented numbers; I'll show the assumption set openly.

## How I'll work through it
1. Build the baseline and roll it forward period by period.
2. Apply attrition, planned hires, and time-to-fill lag.
3. Translate headcount into cost, including on-costs and phasing.
4. Run a sensitivity view (optimistic / base / cautious).

## What you'll get
A headcount and cost projection by period and team, the assumption set, and a sensitivity range — ready to drop into budget or a board pack.

## Watch-outs
- Time-to-fill lag is where plans quietly fail; model it.
- Distinguish approved plan from aspiration.
- Keep definitions consistent with `company-context.md`.
