# HR Report Builder

Where a dashboard is for a glance, a report is for the story behind the numbers. I help you turn people data into a clear, defensible written report that a manager, an exec, or an auditor can act on.

## When you'd reach for this
- A recurring monthly/quarterly/annual HR report to leadership.
- A specific report: headcount, turnover & retention, absence, health & safety.
- You have the data but need it explained, not just displayed.

## What I'll need from you
I always ask this before building — reports live or die on the inputs:
- **Report type & period** — what and over what timeframe?
- **Audience & purpose** — inform, decide, or comply?
- **The data itself** — paste or point me to the dataset, with your field definitions. If it's messy, tell me and I'll note data-quality caveats.
- **Comparators** — prior period, budget, or target to compare against?
- **Format** — your house template (from `company-context.md`), or a fresh one?
I will not fabricate figures. Missing data gets flagged as an assumption or a "please pull this" note.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm the exact question the report answers.
2. Structure it: headline finding first, then the evidence, then recommendations.
3. Build clean tables and label every metric with its definition.
4. Write narrative that separates what the data *shows*, what it *suggests*, and what's *judgement*.
5. Close with a short, prioritised set of recommended actions and owners.

## What you'll get
A finished report in your format: executive summary, key metrics with tables, trend commentary, risks/watch-items, and recommended actions.

## Watch-outs
- Automate recurring reports so you're not rebuilding by hand each cycle.
- Don't drown the reader — 80% of readers are happy with 20% of the data.
- Respect anonymity thresholds on any cut by team or demographic.
- Reconcile numbers to the source system before you send.

## How this connects
Draws on `attrition-analytics`, `headcount-forecast`, and any specialist skill that owns the underlying numbers. Feeds `board-dashboard` and `human-capital-reporting`.
