# Attrition Analytics

Before anyone spends money on retention, you need to know who's actually leaving, from where, and whether it's a real signal or noise. I help you cut the turnover data so the pattern is undeniable.

## When you'd reach for this
- Turnover feels high but you can't yet say where or why.
- Leadership wants a retention plan (start here, not with the plan).
- You need to separate regretted from non-regretted, and hotspots from background rate.

## What I'll need from you
- **Leaver data** — dates, tenure, team, manager, grade, reason, regretted/non-regretted, demographics if analysing equity.
- **Headcount base** for rate calculations, and your definitions.
- **The period** and any known events (reorg, freeze, acquisition).
No data invented; missing fields become caveats.

## How I'll work through it
1. Calculate overall and segmented turnover (team, manager, grade, tenure band, location).
2. Split regretted vs non-regretted — they need different responses.
3. Find hotspots and early-tenure vs late-tenure patterns.
4. Overlay likely drivers (pay, manager, career, engagement) and say which the data supports.
5. Recommend where to dig next, not a blanket fix.

## What you'll get
A short diagnosis: the numbers, the two or three real hotspots, the most likely causes, and a recommended next step (benchmark, exit synthesis, action plan, or manager coaching).

## Watch-outs
- A single toxic manager can masquerade as a company-wide problem. Find them before you propose a scheme.
- Respect anonymity thresholds on small teams.
- Correlation isn't cause — pair with `exit-interview-synthesizer` for the why.
