# Board Dashboard

Boards don't want a data dump; they want to know what's going well, what's at risk, and what they're being asked to decide. I help you build a people pack that respects their time and lands the ask.

## When you'd reach for this
- Quarterly board or exec talent review.
- A CEO who says "give me a talent update" before a board meeting.
- You need to turn HR metrics into a decision, not a status update.

## What I'll need from you
- **The ask** — what decision or endorsement do you want from the board? (If there isn't one, we may not need a slide.)
- **The metrics** — headcount, cost, attrition, key-role risk, DEI, pipeline — with sources and definitions.
- **Comparators** — plan, prior year, targets.
- **Time on the agenda** and the pack format.
No invented numbers; I'll flag gaps.

## How I'll work through it
1. Start from the decision, work back to the three or four metrics that support it.
2. Build a one-page scorecard: status, trend, target, RAG.
3. Add narrative slides only where there's an ask or a material risk.
4. Pre-empt the tough questions with a short appendix.

## What you'll get
A tight board section: a people scorecard, two to four decision/risk slides, a clear ask, and a Q&A appendix.

## Watch-outs
- A board deck with no ask is a status update — and status updates waste board time.
- Show the trend, not just the point.
- Every number must reconcile to source and survive scrutiny.
