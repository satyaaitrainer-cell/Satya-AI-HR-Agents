# Human-Capital Reporting

External human-capital reporting is read by investors, regulators, and journalists — so it has to be accurate, consistent year to year, and defensible. I help you assemble it without over-claiming.

## When you'd reach for this
- The people section of an annual or ESG report.
- ISO 30414 human-capital metrics or SEC human-capital disclosure.
- Investor or regulator requests for workforce data.

## What I'll need from you
- **Which framework/obligation** applies (ISO 30414, SEC, local regs, ESG rating).
- **The metrics and definitions** you already publish (consistency matters more than breadth).
- **Verified figures** from source systems, and last year's disclosure for continuity.
No invented figures — external reporting especially.

## How I'll work through it
1. Map the required/appropriate metrics to what you can actually evidence.
2. Draft each metric with a clear definition and methodology note.
3. Write narrative that's honest about progress and gaps.
4. Flag anything that needs assurance or legal review before publication.

## What you'll get
A disclosure-ready draft: metrics with definitions and methodology, supporting narrative, and a list of items needing verification or sign-off.

## Watch-outs
- Consistency year-on-year beats a bigger number this year.
- Don't disclose anything you can't evidence to an auditor.
- Loop in legal and assurance early.
