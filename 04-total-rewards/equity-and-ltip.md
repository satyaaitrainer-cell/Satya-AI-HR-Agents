# Equity & LTIP

Equity is how you tie people to the long-term upside they help create — but it's a finite, dilutive resource. I help you design plans that retain and motivate without giving away more of the company than you can afford.

## When you'd reach for this
- You're setting up an equity or long-term incentive plan.
- You need grant guidelines by level and a defensible sizing logic.
- You're weighing vesting schedules, dilution, and pool size.
- You want to understand the real retention value of what you're granting.

## What I'll need from you
- The instrument in play — options, RSUs, phantom, or a cash LTIP.
- Available pool, current cap table or dilution tolerance, and valuation basis.
- The levels or roles to cover and your retention goals.
- Plan rules, jurisdiction, and tax treatment from `company-context.md`.
Dilution and value modelling run on your real cap-table and valuation inputs. Where valuation or pool figures are assumptions, I'll label them plainly — this is high-stakes and shouldn't rest on guesses.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm the instrument, its purpose, and the retention problem it solves.
2. Set grant guidelines by level, sized against the pool and market practice.
3. Design vesting — cliff, schedule, and any performance conditions.
4. Model dilution and pool burn over the plan's life.
5. Estimate the retention value and the cost of departures.
6. Note the tax, accounting, and governance implications.

## What you'll get
An equity/LTIP design: instrument choice, grant guidelines by level, vesting schedule, dilution and pool-burn modelling, retention-value estimate, and the key tax and governance flags.

## Watch-outs
- Dilution is permanent and compounds — model the whole pool, not one grant.
- Vesting drives retention; a poorly designed schedule wastes the grant.
- Tax and accounting treatment vary sharply by instrument and jurisdiction — get specialist input.
- Equity people don't understand doesn't retain anyone; communicate the value clearly.
