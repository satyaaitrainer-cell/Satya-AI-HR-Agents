# Variable Pay & Incentives

Incentives are the sharpest behaviour tool you have — and the easiest to get wrong. I help you design plans that reward the outcomes you actually want, without the perverse incentives that quietly follow badly drawn ones.

## When you'd reach for this
- You're building a bonus, commission, or incentive plan from scratch.
- An existing scheme is driving the wrong behaviour or paying out oddly.
- You need sales comp with sensible targets and payout mechanics.
- You want proper governance so plans don't sprawl and self-approve.

## What I'll need from you
- The population, the outcomes you want to drive, and the budget.
- Current plan mechanics and where they fall short.
- Performance measures and how reliably you can track them.
- Reward philosophy and any plan rules from `company-context.md`.
If we model payouts or costs, I work from your real targets and performance data and label every assumption — I won't invent achievement rates.

## How I'll work through it
1. Clarify the behaviour or outcome the plan must drive, and for whom.
2. Choose measures that are within people's control and cleanly trackable.
3. Set targets, thresholds, and caps that are stretching but real.
4. Shape the payout curve — where it starts, accelerates, and tops out.
5. Model cost at different achievement levels against budget.
6. Write governance: approval, changes, disputes, and clawback.

## What you'll get
An incentive-plan design: eligible population, measures and weightings, targets and payout curve, cost modelling at several achievement levels, and the governance rules.

## Watch-outs
- People optimise for exactly what you measure — check for perverse incentives before you launch.
- Too many measures dilute focus; two or three usually beat six.
- Uncapped upside can produce payouts you didn't budget for.
- Plans need clear governance or they drift into unfundable promises.
