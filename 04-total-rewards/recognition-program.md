# Recognition Program

Recognition is one of the cheapest, most under-used levers in reward — done well, it makes people feel seen and reinforces exactly the behaviours you want more of. I help you design a program that's genuine, not gimmicky.

## When you'd reach for this
- People feel their work goes unnoticed and engagement is slipping.
- You want to reinforce specific values or behaviours, not just tenure.
- You're building peer-to-peer or manager-led recognition.
- An existing scheme feels tokenistic and nobody engages with it.

## What I'll need from you
- The values or behaviours you want recognition to reinforce.
- Your culture, team shape, and any current recognition practice.
- Budget, if any — plenty of good recognition costs little.
- Values and norms from `company-context.md` to keep it authentic.
Recognition has to fit your culture; I'll design to it rather than drop in a generic points app, and flag where a proposed mechanic might feel hollow.

## How I'll work through it
1. Pin down the behaviours and values recognition should reinforce.
2. Choose the mechanics — peer, manager, milestone, or a mix.
3. Design rewards that feel meaningful, cash-light where it fits.
4. Make giving and receiving recognition easy and visible.
5. Set light governance to keep it fair and prevent gaming.
6. Plan launch and the rhythm that keeps it alive past month one.

## What you'll get
A recognition-program design: the behaviours it reinforces, the mechanics, reward options, fairness guardrails, and a launch-and-sustain plan.

## Watch-outs
- Recognition that feels forced or corporate backfires — keep it genuine.
- If only a favoured few ever get recognised, it corrodes trust; watch for bias.
- Cash isn't always the answer; specific, timely acknowledgement often lands harder.
- Programs fade without upkeep — design the sustain, not just the launch.

## How this connects
Complements the cash side in `variable-pay-and-incentives` and the package in `benefits-designer`. Works closely with engagement diagnostics, since recognition is one of the most direct levers on how valued people feel.
