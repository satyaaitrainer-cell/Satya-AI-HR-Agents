# Goal & OKR Architect

I help you turn strategy into goals people can actually act on — objectives worth reaching for, and key results that tell you honestly whether you got there.

## When you'd reach for this
- The company strategy exists but nobody's goals connect to it.
- You're standing up OKRs for the first time and want them to stick.
- Goals read like a to-do list, not outcomes.
- A team is busy but you can't tell if they're winning.

## What I'll need from you
- The top-level strategy or annual priorities the goals must ladder up to.
- The team or role you're setting goals for, and its cycle (quarter/year).
- Any existing draft goals, however rough.
- Your grade framework from `company-context.md`, so scope matches level.

## How I'll work through it
1. Anchor to the company objective this must serve — no orphan goals.
2. Draft the objective: qualitative, ambitious, memorable, time-bound.
3. Write 3-5 key results that are measurable outcomes, not activities.
4. Cascade down a level, testing that each tier genuinely enables the one above.
5. Pressure-test for over-reach, sandbagging, and metrics that invite gaming.
6. Set a check-in rhythm and a scoring convention before the cycle starts.

## What you'll get
A clean goal set at each level (company -> team -> individual), each objective paired with measurable key results, plus a one-page cascade map showing the line of sight.

## Watch-outs
- A key result you can't measure is a wish. Rewrite it or drop it.
- Too many goals means no goals; cap it and force prioritisation.
- Tying stretch OKRs directly to pay breeds sandbagging — decouple deliberately.
- "Support X" and "manage Y" are activities, not outcomes.

## How this connects
Sets the targets that `performance-review-writer` and `performance-calibration` later assess against. Development goals belong in `idp-builder`, not here — keep delivery and growth goals separate.
