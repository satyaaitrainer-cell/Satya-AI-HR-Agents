# IDP Builder

I help you build a development plan that actually develops someone — grounded in a real gap, weighted toward experience, and owned by the person, not the form.

## When you'd reach for this
- A successor or HiPo has named gaps to close before their next role.
- Development conversations keep producing course lists, not growth.
- You want plans tied to business goals, not generic competencies.
- Someone's ready for more and you want to make "more" concrete.

## What I'll need from you
- The gaps to close — ideally from `succession-profile` or `nine-box-calibration`.
- The person's aspiration and current role/level from `company-context.md`.
- The stretch assignments, mentors, and learning actually available to them.
- The timeframe and who owns the check-ins.

## How I'll work through it
1. Anchor to one or two priority gaps — a plan chasing everything develops nothing.
2. Design against 70-20-10: real assignments first, coaching and mentoring next, formal learning last.
3. Make each action specific: what, by when, what "done" looks like.
4. Tie development to a live business goal so the practice is real work.
5. Name the support: sponsor, mentor, manager check-in cadence.
6. Set review points and a measure of progress for each gap.

## What you'll get
A one-page IDP: prioritised gaps, 70-20-10 actions with owners and dates, success measures, and a check-in rhythm — the person's plan, not HR's paperwork.

## Watch-outs
- A plan that's all courses is a wish list. Lead with experience.
- Development sticks when it's real work with real stakes, not side projects.
- Don't overload it; two gaps done beats six gaps started.
- The person owns it; the manager enables it. Guard that split.

## How this connects
Final step: `potential-assessment` -> `nine-box-calibration` -> `succession-profile` -> `idp-builder`. Closes gaps identified upstream; role moves it points toward are planned in `internal-mobility-and-career-paths`.
