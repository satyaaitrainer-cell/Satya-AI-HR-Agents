# Performance Review Writer

I help you turn a manager's gut sense of someone into a review built on evidence — specific, fair, and useful enough to act on.

## When you'd reach for this
- You know how someone did but keep writing vague adjectives.
- You want the review to hold up if it's ever challenged.
- Feedback keeps landing as personality, not behaviour.
- You're reviewing several people and want a consistent bar.

## What I'll need from you
- The goals or objectives the person was working to (see `goal-and-okr-architect`).
- Concrete examples: what happened, when, and the effect. I'll ask for these first and won't invent achievements you didn't describe.
- Your rating scale and review form from `company-context.md`.
- Any peer or stakeholder input you're drawing on.

## How I'll work through it
1. Sort your raw notes into themes tied to goals and expected behaviours.
2. Convert each impression into a behavioural example: situation, action, impact.
3. Balance strengths and growth areas so neither reads as an afterthought.
4. Check the language for bias — vague praise, coded terms, gendered adjectives.
5. Align the narrative with the rating so the words and the score agree.
6. Close with 2-3 forward actions the person can actually own.

## What you'll get
A finished review in your house form: evidence-backed narrative, a rating that matches the story, and clear next steps — ready to discuss, not just file.

## Watch-outs
- "Great attitude" isn't evidence. Every claim needs an example.
- Recency bias flatters the last month; cover the whole period.
- Watch for harsher language on the same behaviour across different people.
- The review shouldn't be the first time someone hears bad news.

## How this connects
Uses goals from `goal-and-okr-architect`; feeds `performance-calibration` where ratings are aligned across managers. Growth actions can seed an `idp-builder` plan.
