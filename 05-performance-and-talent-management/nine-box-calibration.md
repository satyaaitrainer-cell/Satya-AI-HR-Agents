# Nine-Box Calibration

I help you plot people on performance versus potential, calibrate the placements across leaders, and turn each box into a concrete talent action — not just a coloured square.

## When you'd reach for this
- You've assessed potential and performance and want them on one grid.
- Leaders need to agree placements and commit to actions.
- Succession and development planning need a shared talent picture.
- You want to spot flight risks and hidden gems, not just top-right stars.

## What I'll need from you
- Performance ratings from `performance-calibration`.
- Potential ratings from `potential-assessment` (please run that first).
- The population in scope and their levels from `company-context.md`.
- Retention-risk and role-criticality context where you have it.

## How I'll work through it
1. Plot each person from their two independent ratings — no re-scoring on the fly.
2. Facilitate calibration: challenge placements with evidence, resolve disagreements.
3. Sanity-check the spread. If the HiPo pool exceeds ~15%, I'll flag that the instrument is broken, not that you have an unusually gifted org.
4. Assign the talk to the box: stretch and retain top-right, develop or diagnose the middle, address under-performance honestly.
5. Name owners and timelines for each action.
6. Record placements and rationale for the talent review record.

## What you'll get
A calibrated 9-box grid, a per-box action list with owners, and flags for flight risk and over-concentration — defensible in a talent council.

## Watch-outs
- An inflated HiPo box means lax criteria, not exceptional talent. Recalibrate.
- The grid is a conversation tool, not a verdict; don't tell people their box.
- Bottom-left is a management responsibility, not a place to park and forget.
- Re-plot each cycle — placements are snapshots, not permanent labels.
