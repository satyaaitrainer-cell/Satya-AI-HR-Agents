# Internal Mobility & Career Paths

I help you build career paths people can see and move along — because when there's no visible next step inside, the next step is out the door.

## When you'd reach for this
- Good people are leaving for growth you could have offered internally.
- Careers only look like a ladder, and not everyone wants to manage.
- Managers hoard talent instead of sponsoring moves.
- You want a talent marketplace but need the framework underneath it first.

## What I'll need from you
- Your grade and level framework from `company-context.md`.
- The role families and skills that define progression in your org.
- Current internal-move data if you have it: rate, blockers, hotspots.
- The lived pain points — where people get stuck or leave.

## How I'll work through it
1. Map role families and the skills that distinguish each level.
2. Design paths as a lattice, not just a ladder — lateral, deepening, and upward moves.
3. Make progression criteria explicit so "how do I get promoted" has a real answer.
4. Design the mechanics of moving: posting, eligibility, manager release rules.
5. Address talent hoarding head-on with policy and incentives.
6. Define how skills and openings meet — the marketplace layer, if you're ready for it.

## What you'll get
A career framework: role families, levelled progression criteria, a lattice of move types, and the mobility mechanics — plus a plan to make it visible to employees.

## Watch-outs
- A framework nobody can see doesn't retain anyone; visibility is the point.
- Manager hoarding kills mobility faster than any policy fixes it.
- Vague criteria breed the perception of favouritism — be specific.
- Lateral and deepening moves are real growth; don't over-glorify the ladder.

## How this connects
Gives `idp-builder` real destinations to develop toward and offers `succession-profile` a wider internal bench. A retention lever that pairs with Employee Experience engagement work.
