# Potential Assessment

I help you judge who can grow into bigger, more complex roles — which is a different question from who's performing well today. Confuse the two and you promote your best doer into a job they'll struggle in.

## When you'd reach for this
- You're preparing for 9-box or succession and need a clean potential read.
- "High performer" and "high potential" are being used interchangeably.
- You want a consistent definition of potential across the org.
- A talent review is coming and gut-feel nominations won't survive scrutiny.

## What I'll need from you
- The people in scope and their roles/levels from `company-context.md`.
- Evidence against each potential signal — not just a manager's hunch.
- Current performance ratings, kept separate so they don't bleed into the potential call.
- Any assessment inputs you have: 360s, mobility history, stretch assignments.

## How I'll work through it
1. Set the definition: aspiration (do they want it), ability (can they learn it), engagement (will they stay and give it).
2. Gather evidence for each dimension, role by role.
3. Rate potential independently of current performance, and say so explicitly.
4. Challenge halo effects, similarity bias, and "just like me" nominations.
5. Distinguish "not yet" from "not ever" — potential is about trajectory.
6. Summarise each person with the evidence behind the call.

## What you'll get
A potential rating per person across aspiration, ability, and engagement, with supporting evidence and explicit separation from performance — ready to plot on the grid.

## Watch-outs
- High performance is not high potential. Say it out loud in every session.
- Aspiration matters: don't label someone HiPo who doesn't want the climb.
- Bias loves this exercise — check who's being overlooked, not just chosen.
- Potential is a forecast, not a promise; revisit it each cycle.

## How this connects
First step in the talent sequence: `potential-assessment` -> `nine-box-calibration` -> `succession-profile` -> `idp-builder`. Assess before you calibrate. Draws performance data from `performance-calibration`, kept as a separate axis.
