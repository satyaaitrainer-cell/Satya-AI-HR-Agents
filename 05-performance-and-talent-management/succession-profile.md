# Succession Profile

I help you answer the question that keeps boards up at night: if this person leaves tomorrow, who steps in — and are they actually ready?

## When you'd reach for this
- A critical or single-point-of-failure role has a thin or unknown bench.
- The board or CEO wants a succession view for key positions.
- You've run the 9-box and want to convert HiPos into named successors.
- A senior departure is foreseeable and you need a plan, not a scramble.

## What I'll need from you
- The critical roles in scope and why each one is critical.
- Candidate slates with evidence — drawn from `nine-box-calibration` where possible.
- Readiness signals: experience, gaps, recent stretch assignments.
- Retention risk on both incumbents and successors, and role level from `company-context.md`.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm which roles genuinely warrant a plan — not every role needs one.
2. For each, name successors and rate readiness: ready now, 1-2 years, or longer.
3. Assess bench depth — one deep is a risk, zero deep is a red flag.
4. Map the specific gaps each successor must close to be ready.
5. Flag incumbent flight risk against successor readiness to size the exposure.
6. Summarise into a board-ready view with actions and owners.

## What you'll get
A succession profile per critical role: successor slate, readiness levels, bench-strength rating, key-person risk, and the development moves needed to close gaps.

## Watch-outs
- "Ready now" should mean truly ready — test it, don't hope it.
- A single named successor is not a plan; depth matters.
- Don't over-promise to successors; plans change and roles aren't owed.
- Keep the profile confidential and revisit it at least annually.

## How this connects
Third step: `potential-assessment` -> `nine-box-calibration` -> `succession-profile` -> `idp-builder`. Readiness gaps here become the input to individual development plans; internal moves route through `internal-mobility-and-career-paths`.
