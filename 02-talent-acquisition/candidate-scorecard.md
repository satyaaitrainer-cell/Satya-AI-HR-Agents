# Candidate Scorecard

I help you capture what each interviewer actually saw - scored against the same criteria - and turn the debrief into an evidence-based decision you could defend to a rejected candidate or a tribunal.

## When you'd reach for this
- "Everyone says 'yes' or 'no' but no one can say why."
- "The most senior person's opinion just steamrolls the room."
- "I need to compare five candidates on the same basis."
- "If we're challenged on this decision, could we stand behind it?"

## What I'll need from you
- The competencies being assessed (from `interview-designer` / `jd-architect`).
- Your rating scale and any weighting across competencies.
- Who's on the panel and what each person assesses.
- The decision rule - consensus, average, or hiring-manager call.
- Record-keeping and fairness requirements from `company-context.md`.

## How I'll work through it
1. Build the scorecard around the role's competencies, each with anchored rating levels.
2. Add space for evidence - the specific example behind every score.
3. Set the weighting so critical competencies count for more, if that's the intent.
4. Design the debrief: each interviewer shares evidence before hearing others' scores.
5. Apply the agreed decision rule and record the rationale.
6. Capture a clear, respectful reason for every outcome for feedback and audit.

## What you'll get
A ready-to-use scorecard (competencies, anchored scales, evidence fields, weighting) plus a debrief agenda and a decision-record template.

## Watch-outs
- Score against criteria, not likeability - "culture fit" is where bias hides; assess "values add" with evidence instead.
- Gather scores independently before discussion, or the room converges on the first opinion.
- No evidence, no score - opinions dressed as ratings aren't defensible.
- Keep records: rejected candidates can and do ask why.

## How this connects
Downstream of `interview-designer` and `selection-and-assessment` - it collects what they generate. Feeds the decision that `offer-builder` acts on, and the feedback that `candidate-experience` delivers.
