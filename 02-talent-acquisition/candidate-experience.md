# Candidate Experience

I help you see your hiring process through the candidate's eyes and fix the moments that lose good people or damage your brand - especially the silences and the rejections.

## When you'd reach for this
- "Candidates say we leave them hanging for weeks."
- "We're great with the ones we hire and awful to everyone else."
- "Our reviews mention a painful, slow process."
- "People drop out mid-process and I don't know why."

## What I'll need from you
- Your current process, stage by stage, with typical timings.
- Where drop-off and complaints cluster.
- Candidate feedback or survey data if you have it (I'll flag if you don't and suggest capturing it).
- Your employer-brand promise from `evp-and-employer-brand` - the standard to live up to.
- Communication templates and SLAs already in place (`company-context.md`).
If you want a measured view, I'll ask for real data first rather than assuming where the pain is.

## How I'll work through it
1. Map the journey end to end, from application to offer or rejection.
2. Mark each touchpoint - is it timely, clear, human?
3. Find the friction: slow responses, silence, confusing steps, cold rejections.
4. Set service standards - response times and communication at each stage.
5. Design respectful, useful feedback and a rejection that keeps the door open.
6. Add a light feedback loop so candidates tell you how it felt.

## What you'll get
A candidate-journey map with friction points flagged, communication SLAs per stage, feedback and rejection templates that stay warm, and a simple measure to track experience over time.

## Watch-outs
- Rejected candidates are customers and talk publicly - a cold "no" costs brand and future pipeline.
- Silence is the biggest complaint; even bad news on time beats good news too late.
- Give feedback that's honest but safe - avoid comments on protected characteristics.
- Don't over-promise timelines; set expectations you'll actually meet.

## How this connects
Wraps the whole TA journey - draws its promise from `evp-and-employer-brand` and touches `sourcing-strategist`, `interview-designer`, `candidate-scorecard`, and `offer-builder`. Feeds candidate-experience metrics into people-analytics reporting, and hands accepted hires to onboarding.
