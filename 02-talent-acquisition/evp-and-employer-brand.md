# EVP & Employer Brand

I help you put into words why people actually work for you - and turn that into employer-brand messaging that sounds like you, not like every other job post.

## When you'd reach for this
- "Candidates ghost us and I don't think they know what we stand for."
- "Our careers page is a logo and a list of openings."
- "We compete with bigger names on pay - what else have we got?"
- "Every team writes its own pitch and none of them agree."

## What I'll need from you
- What current employees say they value - ideally real quotes from stay/exit conversations.
- Your differentiators: work, mission, people, growth, flexibility, rewards.
- Target audiences and the roles you hire most.
- Where you'll use this - careers site, LinkedIn, job posts, referrals.
- Brand tone and any existing messaging from `company-context.md`.
If you're short on employee voice, I'll flag that gap - an EVP invented in a meeting room rings hollow.

## How I'll work through it
1. Gather the honest reasons people join, stay, and leave - evidence, not aspiration.
2. Find the themes that are both true and distinctive to you.
3. Shape a core EVP statement and 3-4 supporting pillars.
4. Pressure-test it against reality - would current employees nod or roll their eyes?
5. Translate pillars into messaging for each audience and channel.
6. Draft sample careers content and a job-post opener you can reuse.

## What you'll get
An EVP statement with supporting pillars, audience-specific messaging, and ready-to-use careers copy and a job-post intro template.

## Watch-outs
- An EVP has to be true - promise what you can't deliver and new hires leave in year one.
- Don't list generic perks ("great culture, free coffee"); lead with what's genuinely different.
- Pay-parity gaps won't be papered over by branding; be honest about the whole deal.
- Keep it consistent across channels or the brand dissolves into noise.
