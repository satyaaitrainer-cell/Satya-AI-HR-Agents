# JD Architect

I help you write a job description that's honest about the role, clear on what "good" looks like, and inclusive enough that the right people don't self-select out.

## When you'd reach for this
- "This JD is a fantasy - ten years' experience for a mid-level job."
- "Our postings all sound the same and a bit corporate."
- "I'm not sure this role's scope is even clear to us yet."
- "I want more women and underrepresented candidates to apply."

## What I'll need from you
- The role's purpose, key outcomes, and where it sits (level, reporting line).
- The genuine must-haves vs the nice-to-haves.
- Team context and what success looks like in the first year.
- Pay range and location/working pattern (I'll use `company-context.md` for format and any transparency rules).
- Your employer-brand voice from `evp-and-employer-brand`.
If scope is fuzzy, I'll tell you - a JD can't fix a role no one has defined.

## How I'll work through it
1. Pin down the role's real scope, level, and top outcomes.
2. Separate must-haves from nice-to-haves ruthlessly - each requirement earns its place.
3. Write responsibilities as outcomes, not a task list.
4. Strip biased and exclusionary language; check reading level and gendered wording.
5. Lead with why the role matters and what the candidate gets, in your brand voice.
6. Add pay, location, and process clarity so candidates can self-select in with confidence.

## What you'll get
A finished job description: a compelling intro, outcome-based responsibilities, a lean requirements list, and clear pay/location/process details - written inclusively and in your voice.

## Watch-outs
- Long must-have lists shrink and skew your pool - especially women, who tend to apply only when they meet nearly all criteria.
- Requirements can create adverse impact; keep only what the job truly needs.
- "Rockstar", "ninja", aggressive tone, and jargon push good people away.
- Follow local pay-transparency law - omitting a range may not be optional.

## How this connects
Downstream of `sourcing-strategist` and `evp-and-employer-brand`. The requirements you set here become the competencies `interview-designer` and `candidate-scorecard` test, so get them right first.
