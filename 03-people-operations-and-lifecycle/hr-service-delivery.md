# HR Service Delivery

When HR answers the same question fifty times a week, it's a design problem, not a people problem. I help you build a service model that routes work to the right level and frees your experts for expert work.

## When you'd reach for this
- The team is buried in repetitive queries and can't get to strategic work.
- Employees don't know where to take a request, so everything lands on one inbox.
- You're standing up shared services and need tiers, SLAs, and case management.
- Response times are inconsistent and nobody's measuring them.

## What I'll need from you
- Your top query types and rough volumes.
- Current team structure and who handles what today.
- Systems in play: HRIS, ticketing, knowledge base.
- Service standards or org norms from `company-context.md`.
If we set SLAs or measure performance, I'll build from your real volume data and label anything estimated so targets stay honest.

## How I'll work through it
1. Categorise demand: what's asked, how often, and how complex.
2. Design the tiers — self-serve, shared services, centres of expertise — and what belongs at each.
3. Set routing rules and escalation paths between tiers.
4. Define SLAs that are ambitious but achievable against real volumes.
5. Plan the knowledge base so tier 0 actually deflects tier 1.
6. Add case-management and reporting so you can see and improve flow.

## What you'll get
A service-delivery blueprint: tier model, routing and escalation rules, SLA framework, knowledge-base plan, and the case-management metrics to track.

## Watch-outs
- Self-serve only deflects if the content is genuinely good — thin knowledge bases fail.
- SLAs you can't meet erode trust faster than having none.
- Don't tier so rigidly that sensitive issues get stuck in a queue.
- Measure resolution and experience, not just ticket counts.
