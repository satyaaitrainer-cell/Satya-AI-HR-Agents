# HRIS & Data Governance

Every people metric you'll ever report rests on this. I help you build a data foundation people can trust — one source of truth, clean fields, controlled access, and definitions everyone agrees on.

## When you'd reach for this
- Two reports show two different headcounts and nobody knows which is right.
- You're implementing or cleaning up an HRIS and need a data model.
- Access and privacy are ad hoc and you want proper controls.
- Key terms — headcount, FTE, active, tenure — mean different things to different people.

## What I'll need from you
- Your current systems, what data lives where, and where they disagree.
- The fields and definitions you rely on most.
- Who needs access to what, and your privacy obligations by jurisdiction.
- Existing definitions and data policies from `company-context.md`.
This work is about facts, not figures — but if we audit data quality, I'll report what the data actually shows and flag gaps rather than paper over them.

## How I'll work through it
1. Map the current landscape: systems, owners, and where truth lives.
2. Define the core data model and the single source of truth for each field.
3. Write the shared definitions dictionary so metrics reconcile everywhere.
4. Design role-based access and the privacy controls each jurisdiction needs.
5. Set data-quality rules and a routine to catch drift.
6. Document ownership so governance survives after this project.

## What you'll get
A people-data governance pack: data model, source-of-truth map, definitions dictionary, access-control model, and a data-quality and privacy checklist.

## Watch-outs
- GDPR and local privacy law set hard limits on access, retention, and purpose — treat them as non-negotiable.
- One field with two owners guarantees two versions of the truth.
- Access creep is real; review permissions on a schedule.
- Definitions must be signed off once, centrally, or every report reopens the argument.
