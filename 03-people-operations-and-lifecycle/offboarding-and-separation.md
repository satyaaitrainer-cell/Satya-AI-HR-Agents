# Offboarding & Separation

How people leave says as much about you as how they arrive — and exits carry real legal risk. I help you run departures cleanly, fairly, and in a way that holds up to scrutiny.

## When you'd reach for this
- A voluntary resignation you want handled well, including a proper exit interview.
- An involuntary exit that must be fair, documented, and defensible.
- A redundancy or reduction-in-force needing objective selection and consultation.
- You want a final-day checklist and a real knowledge-transfer plan.

## What I'll need from you
- The exit type, reason, and timeline.
- The role, jurisdiction, and any collective or contractual obligations.
- For redundancies: the pool, roles at risk, and business rationale.
- Policies, notice terms, and severance rules from `company-context.md`.
For any selection or scoring, I work from your real criteria and headcount data — I won't invent scores. Missing inputs get flagged, and adverse-impact risk gets checked before anything is final.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm exit type and the legal frame that applies in this jurisdiction.
2. For redundancies, build an objective selection matrix and test it for adverse impact.
3. Map consultation and notice obligations, including WARN-style thresholds.
4. Frame severance and final entitlements against policy and law.
5. Sequence the final-day steps: access, assets, records, and communication.
6. Plan knowledge transfer so the work doesn't leave with the person.

## What you'll get
An exit pack for the case: process steps, a selection matrix where relevant, consultation and notice map, severance framing, final-day checklist, and a knowledge-transfer plan.

## Watch-outs
- Involuntary exits and redundancies are legally sensitive — document the rationale and involve counsel early.
- Selection criteria must be objective and consistently applied, or you invite a discrimination claim.
- WARN-style collective-notice thresholds carry hard deadlines; miss them and penalties follow.
- Dignity matters — how you handle an exit ripples through everyone who stays.
