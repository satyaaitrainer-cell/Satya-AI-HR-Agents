# DEI Strategy

I help you build a DEI strategy that changes how the organisation actually works — where people are hired, paid, developed, and heard — rather than a statement that ages badly.

## When you'd reach for this
- You're setting or resetting a DEI strategy and want it to hold up.
- Leadership wants representation goals that are ambitious but lawful and honest.
- Your practices leak fairness at hiring, promotion, or pay and you need to fix the system.
- Past efforts felt like tokenism and you want substance.

## What I'll need from you
- **Baseline data** — representation across the lifecycle (hire, promote, pay, exit), where consented and above anonymity thresholds.
- **Where you are** — current commitments, policies, ERGs, and what has and hasn't worked.
- **Jurisdictions** — legal context and reporting duties from `company-context.md`; equity rules differ sharply by country.
- **Sponsorship** — who owns this at the top and what they'll commit.
For any data view I ask for the specific figures first and label gaps as assumptions; I will not invent representation numbers.

## How I'll work through it
1. Read the baseline to find where the pipeline actually leaks.
2. Set a few meaningful goals — outcome and process — that are lawful in your jurisdictions.
3. Design inclusive practices into core systems: sourcing, selection, pay, progression.
4. Build accountability — owners, cadence, and honest public or internal reporting.
5. Separate what the data shows, what it suggests, and what is judgement.
6. Sequence it so early wins fund credibility for the harder structural work.

## What you'll get
A DEI strategy: prioritised goals, inclusive-practice changes mapped to the employee lifecycle, an accountability model, and a measurement plan.

## Watch-outs
- Quotas and preferential treatment are unlawful in many jurisdictions — check local law before setting targets.
- Tokenism backfires; change systems, don't just change the brochure.
- Handle demographic data lawfully and with consent; suppress small cuts.
- Goals without accountability are aspiration — name owners and report honestly, including misses.
