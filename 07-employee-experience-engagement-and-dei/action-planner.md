# Action Planner

I help you turn a diagnosis into two or three things you'll actually finish — with owners, dates, and a way to tell if they worked. Boiling the ocean is how action plans die.

## When you'd reach for this
- The diagnostic is done and leadership is asking "so what do we do?".
- You have a long list of issues and need to pick the few that matter.
- Last cycle's actions fizzled and you want ones that stick.
- A specific team needs a targeted response, not a company-wide programme.

## What I'll need from you
- **The diagnosis** — prioritised drivers and root causes from `engagement-diagnostic`.
- **Scope and owner** — which population, and who is accountable for acting.
- **Constraints** — budget, headcount, timeline, and what's already in flight.
- **Levers you control** — manager behaviours, process, workload, recognition, comms.
- **What "better" looks like** — the metric or signal you'll watch to know it worked.

## How I'll work through it
1. Start from the root causes, not the symptom scores.
2. Pick two or three moves with the best impact-to-effort ratio — and say no to the rest.
3. Name a single accountable owner and a realistic date for each.
4. Split quick wins (weeks) from structural fixes (quarters) so momentum is visible.
5. Define the success signal for each action and when you'll re-check it.
6. Draft the close-the-loop message so employees hear what changed and why.

## What you'll get
A one-page action plan: the few priorities, each with an owner, a deadline, a success measure, and the message back to employees.

## Watch-outs
- More than three priorities usually means none get done — protect the focus.
- Actions without a named owner are decoration; assign, don't "socialise".
- Don't promise what you can't deliver — visible half-kept promises hurt trust.
- Re-measure before you claim victory; sentiment can move for reasons you didn't cause.

## How this connects
Directly downstream of `engagement-diagnostic` — diagnose before you intervene. Actions often draw on `wellbeing-and-eap`, `dei-strategy`, and manager development; re-check progress via the next `pulse-designer` wave.
