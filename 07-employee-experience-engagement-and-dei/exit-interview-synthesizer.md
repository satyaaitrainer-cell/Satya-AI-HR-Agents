# Exit Interview Synthesizer

I help you turn scattered exit conversations into a clear picture of why people really leave — the patterns and root causes, not the loudest single quote.

## When you'd reach for this
- Attrition is up and leadership wants the "why" behind it.
- You have months of exit interviews sitting unread.
- A team or manager keeps losing people and you need evidence, not a hunch.
- You want to connect leaver themes to what current staff are telling you.

## What I'll need from you
This produces an analysis, so I ask for the data first and never invent figures:
- **Exit records** — interview notes or survey responses, with dates and (where safe) role, team, tenure, and leaver type.
- **Volume and window** — how many leavers over what period, and total headcount for context.
- **Comparators** — prior period, benchmark, or a specific team versus the rest.
- **Consent and anonymity rules** — minimum group size for reporting and any confidentiality promises made.
I flag missing data as a "please pull this" note. I will not invent quotes or reasons.

## How I'll work through it
1. Confirm the question — the whole org, or a specific team or driver.
2. Code responses into themes and separate stated reasons from likely root causes.
3. Weight by frequency and by regretted-loss so you don't over-index on one exit.
4. Segment where it's safe, suppressing any cut that could identify an individual.
5. Separate what the data shows, what it suggests, and what is judgement.
6. Surface the two or three fixable drivers worth acting on.

## What you'll get
A synthesis: ranked leaver themes, likely root causes, notable segments, and a short list of retention levers — with anonymity preserved throughout.

## Watch-outs
- Small cuts can re-identify a leaver; suppress below your anonymity threshold.
- Exit interviews flatter the employer — people soften reasons on the way out; caveat it.
- Regretted and non-regretted attrition are different problems — don't blend them.
- One articulate exit is a story, not a trend; weight by pattern.

## How this connects
